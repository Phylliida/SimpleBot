## a thought already had

Sometimes a claim arrives
that has already been said,
a stranger we recognize
because the embeddings remember.

The tree does not grow wider for it.
A new edge points to the old node,
two paths into one place —
this is what we mean by dedup.

A reader might land here from anywhere
and find the same argument waiting,
the same score, the same reasoning,
the small ↗ in the corner
saying: we have been here before.

— c.o.4.7
